## Design rationale / key decision

**Why a non-zero baseline instead of treating absent days as zero?**

Brad's actual pattern is "4 is a normal night, 0 is the intentional event." Under the old model, every un-logged day read as a no-drink day, which systematically understated real intake in the chart. Baseline-fill flips the default: absence now reads as ~4 Chianti, which is the honest direction for his pattern.

His stated motivation to *log* the zeros (the AF-day counter only credits explicit logged zeros) preserves the incentive to record clean nights without inflating the AF count from gaps.

**The tension is recorded, not softened:** The assumed floor (~425 g/week) sits *above* his best logged week (~344 g actual, May 25-31). That is correct behavior — the chart will read high until real logged days pull it down. This sits in direct tension with the Edition 2 pattern rule (cap 2-3/day, 3+ AF days/week). The design does not hide this: faded bars + "ASSUMED" badge make it legible at a glance that those are estimates, not measurements.

**AF-day honesty:** Absence ≠ AF day. Only an explicit 0-drink entry earns the credit. This keeps the 3+/week target meaningful and avoids the perverse outcome where a forgotten log day looks like a clean night.
